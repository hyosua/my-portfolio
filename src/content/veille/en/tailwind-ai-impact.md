- **Tailwind CSS**, a major **open-source CSS framework** led by **Adam Wathan**, is facing severe challenges due to the rise of **AI-driven developer tools**.

- Since early **2023**, **documentation traffic dropped 40%** as developers increasingly rely on **AI language models** instead of visiting official docs.<br /><br />

- This shift undermined Tailwind’s **documentation-based monetization model**, which promoted **premium products** like **UI kits** and **design templates**.

- As a result, Tailwind suffered an **80% revenue decline**, despite being ranked the **most preferred CSS framework** in the **2025 State of CSS survey**.<br /><br />

- The financial impact forced the company to **lay off 75% of its engineering team**, shrinking from **four engineers to one**.

- The situation highlights a broader **open-source sustainability crisis**, where **AI bypasses traditional engagement and revenue channels**.<br /><br />

- To stabilize the project, **Vercel**, **Supabase**, and **Google AI Studio** pledged **sponsorship support**, alongside **community contributions**.<br /><br />

- Wathan remains **optimistic**, focusing on **new funding models** and **AI-optimized engagement strategies** to ensure Tailwind’s future.
